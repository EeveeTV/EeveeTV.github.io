Guido van Rossum


Guido van Rossum is a famous person for many things. He was born on the 31/01/1956

Early Life Guido was born on the 31/01/1956, at Haarlem, Netherlands being the eldest of the three. He has a brother, Just van Rossum, who is a typographer and programmer who designed the typeface used in the Python logo. Not much is known about his childhood. Family His father was an architect, and his mother trained as a school teacher but left her job after marriage. As stated in Early Life, he has a brother, Just van Rossum. He is the eldest of three children, one being Just van Rossum, his brother, and his sister, name unknown. His parents were a part of the Pacifist and Labor political parties. Work/Career His first job was working at CWI (Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica), the group was working on authoring software for presentations, for operating system and network support. From April 1995 to February 1998, Guido was a guest researcher for the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology. From July 2003 till December 2005, he worked for Elemental Security. From October 2000 till July 2003, he worked for Zope Corporation as Director of PythonLabs. From May through October 2000, he worked for BeOpen.com. 2005 to 2012 he worked at Google, then from 2013 to 2018 he worked at Dropbox Achievements Creating Python Programming Language Dr. Dobb's Journal 1999 Excellence in Programming Award NLUUG Award 2003 And more!
